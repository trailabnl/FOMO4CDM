Foundation Models for Clinical Decision Making

Submit an Abstract

Overview

Foundation models, i.e., large-scale pre-trained systems that generalize across tasks and modalities, are rapidly transforming clinical AI. This session brings together researchers and beyond to examine their application in clinical decision making through three interconnected lenses: representation (learning from heterogeneous clinical data), clinical reasoning (supporting diagnosis and decision-making), and interaction (enabling meaningful human-AI dialogue in clinical workflows).  The session will: (1) showcase state-of-the-art representation learning from multimodal clinical data (EHR, medical imaging, genomics, clinical text); (2) critically examine how foundation models support clinical reasoning, diagnostics, and treatment planning; and (3) explore the design and evaluation of interactive, conversational AI systems deployed in real clinical environments.

The workshop is designed for the full breadth of the BNAIC community: principal investigators, postdoctoral researchers, PhD candidates, master's students in medical and/or AI research groups; clinical practitioners and healthcare informaticians; policymakers and regulatory stakeholders; and interdisciplinary researchers. No specific prior expertise in foundation models is assumed; the session is structured to be accessible and stimulating across levels of seniority.
